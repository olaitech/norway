# Content Roadmap

## Editorial Goal

Turn the visual foundation into a useful Norway travel resource: cinematic at
first glance, credible and practical once the visitor begins planning.

## Content Standards

Every major guide should:

- Open with a strong sense of place and an honest planning premise.
- Explain who the trip or destination suits.
- Include timing, duration, logistics and weather-aware guidance.
- Offer a realistic pace rather than an overfilled checklist.
- Link naturally to related routes, destinations, seasonal advice and maps.
- Use concrete language and real Norwegian spellings.
- Avoid filler, vague superlatives and copied tourism prose.

## Priority Tracks

### 1. High-Intent Planning Pages

Build substantial content for:

- Best time to visit Norway.
- Norway road trip routes.
- Lofoten travel guide.
- Northern lights in Norway.
- Fjords of Norway.
- Norway itinerary: 7 days.
- Norway itinerary: 10 days.

These pages should answer search intent while preserving the documentary tone.
See [seo-pages.md](./seo-pages.md) for page-by-page briefs.

### 2. Destination Depth

Expand current destination pages with practical depth:

| Destination | Content opportunities |
| --- | --- |
| Lofoten | Bases, route pacing, ferry/drive options, seasonal light, village etiquette |
| Senja | Scenic route strategy, access from Tromsø, hiking/weather alternatives |
| Helgeland Coast | Ferry rhythm, island stays, slow-road itineraries |
| Tromsø | Aurora base planning, city/wilderness balance, winter logistics |

Future candidates can be added only when their content is substantial enough to
match the existing brand standard.

### 3. Journal Archive

Develop a premium editorial archive of field-note style articles:

- Road narratives.
- Seasonal light essays.
- Location-specific photography/travel notes.
- Ferry and coastal journey stories.
- Practical companion pieces linked back to planning pages.

Journal writing can be more atmospheric than SEO guides, but should still add
useful context or point toward relevant planning resources.

### 4. Map As Planning Content

The map page should grow beyond a visual feature:

- Connect map locations to destination guides.
- Surface route ideas and arrival gateways.
- Explain regional connections, ferry considerations and travel pacing.
- Keep outbound map links useful for live navigation checks.

## Suggested Guide Template

Use this outline as a starting point, adapted to the subject:

1. Hero: title, intent-focused introduction and evocative real image.
2. Why this matters: what kind of trip or traveller the page serves.
3. Best timing or route overview.
4. Practical sections: transport, nights/bases, weather, driving/ferries or
   equipment where relevant.
5. Curated highlights with restraint.
6. Suggested itinerary or decision framework.
7. Related guides/internal links.
8. Closing planning call to action.

## Internal Linking Strategy

| From | Link naturally to |
| --- | --- |
| Seasonal pages | Northern lights, routes, destination pages |
| Destination guides | Map, matching route, season advice, journal story |
| Route guides | Map, destinations along route, timing advice |
| Journal articles | One or two relevant practical planning pages |
| Map page | Destination details and route guides |

## Editorial Quality Checklist

- Is the content genuinely useful without relying on visuals?
- Does it include a realistic trip pace?
- Are seasonal or logistical claims accurate and updateable?
- Are visible names correctly spelled (`Bodø`, `Tromsø`, `Vesterålen`)?
- Are internal links relevant rather than excessive?
- Does the voice feel calm and informed, not promotional?
- Are facts requiring current verification clearly identified before
  publication?
